## The Lead: A Marketing Agency in a Box

**PolyWiz is a marketing agency in a box.** That's the tagline for Steve. He'll get it immediately.

One person built a system that does what a mid-size social media team does: scrape content, generate platform-specific posts across 13 channels, schedule them over weeks and months with tapering algorithms, manage brand voice across multiple organizations, and publish — with human approval on every post. It runs real campaigns right now for four arts organizations.

Steve will want to call it "AI-powered." And for his world, that framing works — his clients are mid-market companies who respond to that language. But in the 501(c)(3) arts space, there's real skepticism of AI. Arts organizations don't want to hear that a robot is writing their exhibition announcements. So PolyWiz doesn't lead with "AI" — it leads with *sustained presence*, *freeing up your team*, *nothing goes live without your say*. The AI is the engine, not the brand.

**The point for Steve:** This is evidence. A working, running, production system that proves the "AI-automated marketing department" concept isn't theoretical. It exists. I built it. And I built it using the same boilerplate template approach that Jackie could use to build tools for Vista Growth.

## The Shared Philosophy — and Where We Diverge

Both PolyWiz and Vista Growth believe AI frees people up rather than replacing them. But we frame it very differently because our audiences are different:

**Steve's world (mid-market B2B):** "AI-powered" is a selling point. Clients want to hear it. The language of transformation, automation, and AI governance resonates. Steve can and should lean into that.

**My world (501(c)(3) arts organizations):** "AI-powered" triggers skepticism. Arts organizations don't want a robot writing about their artists. So PolyWiz talks about *sustained social media presence*, *freeing your team from content grind*, *human approval on every post*. The intelligence is under the hood, not on the label.

**Same philosophy, different packaging.** This is worth discussing with Steve — it's actually a nuance his clients will face too. When Vista Growth advises a company on AI adoption, the internal messaging ("we're freeing you to do higher-value work") matters as much as the external capability. PolyWiz is a case study in how to deploy AI without leading with "AI."

### What PolyWiz demonstrates in practice:
- We're not eliminating marketing roles — we're eliminating the exhaustion of manually creating dozens of platform-specific posts
- Humans decide what to promote, review every post, approve before publish
- The freed-up time goes to social engagement, community building, and creative strategy
- **For Steve's pitch to his clients:** "What if your marketing department could have entire functions running on autopilot, freeing up marketers to focus on engagement instead of content generation?" PolyWiz is literally that. It runs.

## The Bigger Strategic Point: Don't Recommend Solutions, Advise on Navigation

This is important for the conversation with Steve about Vista Growth's advisory model.

**My take:** This space is moving so fast that recommending specific AI solutions to clients is a losing game. Whatever you recommend in April will be surpassed by July. I'm building PolyWiz for my own arts organizations — but I think of it as an internal project, not a product I'd tell other people to adopt. The landscape of marketing automation, content generation, and social media management tools is exploding. There will be dozens of excellent "marketing agency in a box" solutions within a year.

**What Vista Growth should be advising on instead:**

The structural change. Not "use this tool" but "here's how to navigate the transition":

- **How to redeploy people** when AI takes over content generation — what do your marketers do now? (Engagement, strategy, community, brand)
- **How to restructure teams** — you don't need 5 content writers anymore, but you might need 2 content strategists and 3 community managers
- **How to evaluate and adopt** — not which tool, but what criteria to use, how to pilot, how to measure, how to iterate
- **How to compete better** — the point isn't cost savings (though that happens). The point is that your competitors will have AI-powered marketing departments, and if you don't, you're bringing a knife to a gunfight. This is about competitiveness, not efficiency.
- **How to manage the human side** — retraining, redeployment, morale, the narrative you tell your own people about why this is happening

**PolyWiz as evidence, not prescription:** "I'm not telling you to use PolyWiz. I'm showing you what one person built in months. This is the caliber of tool that will be commonplace. Your clients need to be ready for a world where this exists — and Vista Growth should be the ones helping them get ready."

This positions Steve and Jackie as navigators of structural change rather than tool recommenders — which is more durable, more valuable, and more honest given how fast the landscape shifts.

## Part 2: The Boilerplate Pitch to Jackie

### The Origin Story

Everything started with a YouTube video by **Bart Slodyczka**: ["Build 10X Faster With This Simple Claude Code Workflow"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uHpUVBA8dWE).

Bart created a boilerplate template for building full-stack applications with AI assistance. The key insight: if you give Claude Code a well-structured starting point — authentication, database schema, UI components, API patterns — it can build real applications, not just websites.

### What the Boilerplate Includes

Bart's original repo: [Barty-Bart/nextjs-supabase-shadcn-boilerplate](https://github.com/Barty-Bart/nextjs-supabase-shadcn-boilerplate)

The Polymash forks (customized and extended):
- **Supabase version:** [JuergenB/polymash-nextjs-supabase-shadcn-boilerplate](https://github.com/JuergenB/polymash-nextjs-supabase-shadcn-boilerplate) — Full-stack with Supabase (Postgres, Auth, Storage, Realtime)
- **Airtable version:** [JuergenB/polymash-nextjs-airtable-boilerplate](https://github.com/JuergenB/polymash-nextjs-airtable-boilerplate) — Same architecture but with Airtable as the data layer

**What these templates give you out of the box:**
- Next.js + React + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui
- Authentication and login flows (email, OAuth, magic links)
- Database integration (Supabase or Airtable)
- Dashboard layouts with sidebar navigation
- API route patterns
- Role-based access control foundations
- Deployment-ready for Vercel

### The Key Concepts to Share with Jackie

**1. Feature Prompting**
Instead of writing code line by line, you describe features to Claude Code and it builds them against the boilerplate's architecture. "Add a form that collects company information and stores it in Supabase" becomes a working feature in minutes, not days. The boilerplate provides the patterns; AI fills in the specifics.

**2. Database-Backed Applications**
The jump from "website" to "application" is the database. Jackie built the Vista Growth site with Claude Code — that's great. The next level is connecting it to Supabase or Airtable so it can store data, manage users, track state, and power workflows.

**3. Boilerplate Templates as Accelerators**
Authentication, login sequences, user management, role-based access — these are solved problems baked into the template. You don't prompt Claude to figure out auth from scratch; the boilerplate already has it. Claude extends it with your specific business logic.

**4. Subdomain Apps as Lead Generation**
Imagine Vista Growth's website at `vistagrowth.ai` with a tool living at `audit.vistagrowth.ai` — a self-service AI Transformation assessment that prospects can take before ever talking to Steve or Jackie. That's not a contact form; it's a qualified lead generator. The boilerplate makes building this kind of tool practical for a small team.

## Part 3: The AI Transformation Audit as a Built Tool

### What Vista Growth Has Now

A $7,500 consulting engagement delivered over two weeks:
- Current state assessment of marketing operations
- AI Opportunity Matrix (5-8 high-impact processes ranked by revenue impact, complexity, time-to-results)
- ROI/financial projections
- Technology recommendations
- Implementation roadmap
- 90-day action plan

### What It Could Become

An **interactive self-service assessment tool** that lives at a subdomain and does the following:

**Phase 1 — Qualification & Lead Generation (build first)**
- **Conversational AI interview** (15-20 min, audio-only) that adapts follow-up questions based on responses — NOT a static form. [Research](research-ai-interview-platforms.md) shows conversational AI achieves 70-90% completion vs 10-30% for forms, with 2.5-8x richer response data. Audio-only recommended over video avatars (95% completion vs 40-60%).
- Covers marketing operations, tech stack, team size, current AI usage — but conversationally, with intelligent follow-ups
- AI-scored results with a "transformation readiness" score across multiple pillars
- Personalized recommendations based on responses
- Beautiful PDF report with Vista Growth branding, including key quotes from the interview
- CTA: "Want the full audit? Book a call with Steve and Jackie"
- Data flows into Airtable → CRM for follow-up. Steve and Jackie see full transcript, AI summary, and readiness score before any human call.
- **Recommended platforms:** Start with TheySaid ($49/mo) for validation, build custom with Retell.ai (~$1-1.50/interview) at scale

**Phase 2 — Multi-Participant Organizational Assessment (add next)**
- Multiple stakeholders from the same organization (CMO, Marketing Director, Content Manager) each take the AI interview independently
- System generates per-participant summaries plus an **organizational rollup** — aggregate readiness score, areas of agreement/divergence across participants
- Divergence between stakeholders reveals organizational alignment issues — a consultable finding Steve and Jackie can address
- Scoring rubrics feed into the AI Opportunity Matrix
- See [AI Interview Platforms Research](research-ai-interview-platforms.md) for full platform comparison, pricing, and architecture

**Phase 3 — Full Audit Platform (aspirational)**
- Client portal where audit findings live
- Progress tracking against the 90-day action plan
- Ongoing monitoring and scoring updates

### Why This Matters for Vista Growth's Positioning

Right now they say: "We're AI marketing transformation experts."

With a built tool they say: "We're AI marketing transformation experts — and we built our own AI tools to prove it. Try one."

That's a fundamentally different conversation with prospects. It's the difference between "trust us, we know AI" and "here, experience what we build."

### The Boilerplate Path

Using the Polymash Supabase boilerplate + Claude Code:

1. Clone the boilerplate → instant Next.js app with auth, database, dashboard
2. Integrate conversational AI interview via Retell.ai or Vapi.ai API → prospects experience an adaptive conversation, not a form
3. Feature-prompt the scoring engine → Claude creates the assessment logic against the database schema
4. Feature-prompt the PDF generation → Claude adds branded report output with interview highlights
5. Wire up webhooks → interview transcripts, scores, and summaries flow to Airtable automatically
6. Deploy to Vercel on a subdomain → live in days, not months

Jackie has already proven she can work with Claude Code. The boilerplate is the bridge to building real tools, not just websites.

## Demo Flow (Suggested Order)

1. **Open with the tagline** — "I built a marketing agency in a box. Let me show you what that means." Don't oversell the AI angle — let the system speak for itself. Steve will see the AI; that's his lens. The point is that it *works*.

2. **Marketing site walkthrough** (5 min) — polywiz-marketing.vercel.app. Note how it doesn't scream "AI" — it leads with the problem (one post on opening night, then silence) and the solution (sustained presence with human approval). This is intentional positioning for the arts audience.

4. **The strategic point** (5 min) — "I wouldn't recommend PolyWiz to your clients. I wouldn't recommend any specific tool right now. The space moves too fast. But tools like this will be commonplace within a year. What your clients need isn't a tool recommendation — they need someone to help them navigate what happens when these tools exist. How do you redeploy your content team? How do you restructure around engagement instead of production? That's Vista Growth's real value."

5. **The origin story** (5 min) — "This started with a YouTube video and a boilerplate template. One person built this with AI." Transition to the boilerplate pitch for Jackie.

6. **The boilerplate pitch** (10 min) — show Jackie the repos, explain feature prompting, explain the jump from websites to database-backed applications. The AI Transformation Audit is the obvious first project.

7. **Leave the URLs and the framing** — PolyWiz is evidence of what's possible. The boilerplate is how to get started. Vista Growth's job isn't to build PolyWiz for clients — it's to help clients navigate a world where PolyWiz exists.
