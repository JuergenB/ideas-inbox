Hi Emma,

Thanks for reaching out. I spent some time putting together my thinking on what a podcast website builder actually needs to compete with Podpage and the hosting platforms.

The short version: the market is thin, the existing products are lazy about content, and the structural opportunity is bigger than most people realize. Every current builder just pulls the RSS feed description onto a template page — which is the equivalent of using a movie trailer as the movie. The real value is in the page architecture: what sections an episode page should have, what structured data it should generate, and how the website connects to the promotional lifecycle.

I've attached a deck that walks through:
- The competitive landscape (who's out there, what they charge, where they fall short)
- What podcasters actually pay for (survey data from 558 creators)
- The content philosophy that separates good podcast websites from bad ones
- The specific page structure and features that would differentiate a new product
- The SEO and AI search opportunity most builders are ignoring

This is based on roughly a decade of working in podcast production and discoverability. Happy to discuss further if it's useful.
