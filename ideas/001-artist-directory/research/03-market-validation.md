## Free-to-Paid Transition Strategies

**Key data points:**
- Median free-to-paid conversion rate across SaaS: **8%** (10x variance between top and bottom)
- Freemium generates more total paying customers than free trials despite lower per-user conversion
- Requiring credit card upfront: 25-35% conversion (5x higher) but dramatically reduces signups
- 75% of new SaaS users abandon within the first week without effective onboarding

**Founding member pricing** works well for early-stage platforms:
- Permanent discounts for early adopters create stickiness
- Users are more tolerant of bugs and more willing to give feedback
- Gradual price increases for new members test price elasticity
- Strong fit for nonprofit context: "Support the arts, lock in your rate forever"

## Qualifying Onboarding Sequences

**Best practices from successful platforms:**
- Dribbble: Asks role (designer/team/client) → routes to different product experiences
- LinkedIn: Segments by role → customizes feed and recommendations
- Pinterest: Asks interest categories → personalizes feed immediately → dramatically improves retention
- Patreon: Asks content type and frequency → surfaces different monetization features
- Substack: Segments by publishing format → routes to different monetization approaches

**Recommended questions:**
1. "What best describes you?" — Artist/Maker, Gallery Owner, Curator, Collector, Interior Designer, Art Educator, Art Lover, Other
2. "What brings you here?" — Discover new artists, Find art for a project, Get my art seen, Research artists, Build a collection
3. "How did you hear about us?" — Exhibition partner, Social media, Search, Friend, Art publication, University

**Routing logic:**
- Galleries/curators/collectors → white-glove sequence, personal outreach within 48 hours
- Artists → portfolio-building sequence, invite to submit to exhibitions
- Designers → sourcing tools emphasis, premium tier nudge
- Art lovers → engagement sequence, newsletter signup

**Key finding:** 94% of email replies occur within the first 3 follow-up steps. The onboarding-to-email-sequence pipeline is critical.

## Survey Design: Van Westendorp + MaxDiff

### Section 1: Segmentation (2 min)
- Role question
- Current platform usage
- Frequency of online art browsing

### Section 2: Feature Prioritization — MaxDiff (5-7 min)
Test 12-15 features across ~20 question sets. Each shows 4-5 features; pick MOST and LEAST important.

### Section 3: Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity (3 min)
For each tier:
1. At what price would this be so cheap you'd question quality?
2. At what price would it be a bargain?
3. At what price would it feel expensive but you'd still consider?
4. At what price would it be too expensive?

Plus Newton-Miller-Smith extension: "How likely would you purchase at [bargain price]?"

### Section 4: Open-ended (2 min)
- "What would make you willing to pay for an art discovery platform?"
- "What's the single most valuable thing an art platform could do for you?"

**Critical:** Segment all results by role. Aggregate averages are meaningless.

## The 90-Day Experiment

| Week | Activity | Go/No-Go Threshold |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Launch simple public gallery | Baseline traffic |
| 2-3 | Email signup gate + qualifying questions | **15%+ signup = promising** |
| 3-4 | Monitor engagement | **5+ min = strong engagement** |
| 4-5 | Van Westendorp survey to 1,000+ artists | **200+ responses needed** |
| 5-6 | Smoke test: pricing pages + $300-600 ads | **8%+ CTR = strong signal** |
| 6-8 | Segment analysis by role | Any segment showing $5+/mo WTP |
| 8-9 | 15-20 qualitative interviews | Explicit "I would pay" statements |
| 9-12 | Synthesize and decide | See framework below |

### Decision Framework

**Build paid version if:** signup >15%, any segment WTP $5+/mo in survey AND smoke test, qualitative interviews confirm

**Keep as lead magnet if:** strong engagement but weak WTP across all segments

**Rethink if:** signup <10%, time on site <2 min, survey response <10% from existing artists

**Total cost:** $300-600 ad spend + staff time. No engineering required if using no-code for initial gallery.

## Nonprofit-Specific Advantages

**Trust:** Nonprofit status significantly increases willingness to engage. Shifts psychology from "Am I getting a deal?" to "Am I supporting something worthwhile?"

**Grant opportunities:**
| Funder | Program | Amount |
|--------|---------|--------|
| NEA | Grants for Arts Projects | $10K-$100K |
| Knight Foundation | Arts Program | Varies (specifically funds digital cultural infrastructure) |
| Bloomberg Philanthropies | Arts Program | Varies |
| Ford Foundation | Creativity & Free Expression | Varies |

**Tax implications:**
- Subscription revenue is likely "related income" (not UBTI) if documented as mission-aligned
- Data licensing revenue more complex — selling raw contact data could trigger UBTI and private benefit concerns
- Board documentation essential for monetization decisions
