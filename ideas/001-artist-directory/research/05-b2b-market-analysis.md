# Research: B2B Art Market Analysis & Fit Assessment

**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Source:** Perplexity `sonar-deep-research`
**Cost:** ~$0.74

---

## How Galleries Currently Find Artists

- Other artists (peer referrals) — the #1 channel
- Instagram browsing — visual portfolio + social proof
- Art fairs and group exhibitions
- MFA graduation shows
- Word of mouth through existing networks
- Cold outreach from artists (low success rate)

**The honest truth:** Galleries have an **oversupply problem**, not a discovery problem. The contemporary art auction market dropped 36% in 2024 to its lowest since 2018.

**Small gallery economics:**
- Median revenue: ~$519K/year
- Median owner earnings: ~$149K
- Art fair costs: $20K+ per fair, losing money 15-50% of the time
- Tech spending: $200-500/year on specialized software
- Maximum realistic subscription: $25/mo ($300/year)

---

## Curators and Exhibition Organizers

**How curators find artists for themed shows:**
- Personal networks built over years
- Academic literature and previous exhibition records
- Artsy and similar platforms
- Social media scanning
- Curator-to-curator referrals

**A filtered database would genuinely help** for curators building shows around themes — searching by medium + subject + tags + location maps to curatorial workflow.

**Market size:** ~2,500-4,000 independent curators in North America. Museum curators have institutional budgets ($2K-5K/year justifiable) but independents are price-sensitive ($200-500/year).

**TAM for curator segment:** $7.5-12M nationally. Realistic capture: $40K-100K/year.

---

## Art Publishers and Editorial

Would find value in pre-written profiles and pre-cleared images as a production efficiency tool. But:
- Tiny market: 100-200 major publications globally, 500-1,000 online
- Thin budgets
- Willingness to pay: $300-3,000/year

**Better model:** Partnership over subscription — free database access in exchange for featuring artists with attribution links.

---

## Interior Designers and Art Consultants — THE REAL OPPORTUNITY

**A genuine, expensive pain point:**
- Sourcing 12 paintings at 36"x48" with specific color palettes = 20-30 hours manual browsing
- Existing platforms lack multi-dimensional filtering (size + medium + subject + color + price)
- No price transparency — must contact each artist/gallery individually
- Assessing professionalism and delivery reliability is time-consuming

**Asset-to-need match:**
- 8,000 artworks with **dimensions** (AI-extracted) — gold for designers
- Medium and subject matter classification — enables spec-driven search
- AI tags for visual characteristics
- Artist profiles indicating professionalism/exhibition history
- Ongoing intake pipeline = growing inventory

**The economics work:**
- Interior design services market: $128B globally (2024)
- Wall art market: $67B (2025), North America = 44% share
- Real technology budgets, healthy margins
- Willingness to pay: $300-500/month
- ~10,000-15,000 commercial design firms in the US with scale
- Plus several thousand independent art consultants

**What needs adding:**
- Price/budget range information
- Color palette filtering (AI could extract dominant colors)
- Availability/commission status
- Shipping/logistics info
- Higher-resolution image access

**Realistic revenue:** $150K-350K/year near-term.

## Corporate Art Programs

Hotels, hospitals, offices, restaurants — significant spend ($50K to millions per project) but almost never source directly. They work through art consultants/advisors (~1,500-3,500 in US).

**Don't sell to corporations. Sell to the art consultants who serve them.**

Realistic revenue: $45K-100K/year from 15-25 firms at $3K-5K/year.

---

## Arts Education

Genuine use cases (curriculum, guest lectures, partnerships) but:
- Rigid budget cycles (6-12 month approval)
- Requires consortia model (like JSTOR at $4K-40K/year)
- 24-36 months minimum to viable revenue

**Better strategy:** Free access for MFA programs. Students become future gallery owners, curators, designers who know the platform.

## Competitive Landscape

| Platform | B2B Pricing | Value Prop | Gap |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| **Artsy** | Negotiated per gallery; ~20% commission | Digital storefront, CMS, collector data | Gallery infrastructure, not artist discovery for buyers |
| **Artnet** | $500-3K/mo (analytics) | Market data, auction results | Established/secondary market focus |
| **Artwork Archive** | $99-500/mo | Portfolio management | Artist-facing, not buyer-facing |
| **ArtFacts** | ~€50-200/mo | Artist rankings, exhibitions | Rankings-focused, not visual search |
| **Artlogic** | ~£500-1,500/mo | Gallery management | Operations software |

**The gap:** No platform combines AI-enriched discovery + visual attribute search + emerging focus + nonprofit credibility + price transparency.

## Key Takeaway

1,000 artists / 8,000 artworks is a start, not a moat. Scaling to 5,000+ artists with 25,000+ artworks (with comprehensive dimension, price, and availability data) is when this becomes compelling to professional buyers. The AI enrichment pipeline — the ability to scale data efficiently — is the real competitive advantage.
